2\. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY



Over the course of this eight-week internship, I served as a Gen AI Engineer on the CreditSentinel project, an Artificial Intelligence–powered credit risk intelligence platform designed to support underwriting operations for Indian Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs). My primary responsibility was to strengthen the reliability, quality, and operational readiness of the platform's memorandum generation system powered by the Groq Large Language Model (LLM).



Rather than focusing solely on model integration, my work emphasized transforming Generative Artificial Intelligence into a dependable production component capable of producing structured, explainable, and business-ready underwriting memoranda. This required combining prompt engineering, systematic quality assurance, production validation, performance benchmarking, and comprehensive technical documentation to ensure that every generated memorandum met organizational standards for consistency, factual accuracy, and readability.



One of my most significant contributions was the design and implementation of a measurable quality assurance framework for memorandum generation. Instead of relying on subjective review, I established objective evaluation criteria covering factual consistency, structural completeness, recommendation quality, readability, latency, and hallucination detection. These quality gates enabled systematic validation across more than 15,000 generated memoranda, resulting in a validated 5.0/5 quality score, 100% structural completeness, and zero observed hallucinations during evaluation.



Production readiness formed another major aspect of my internship. I participated in validating Service Level Agreements (SLAs) by measuring memorandum generation latency, API responsiveness, system availability, and operational reliability. Performance validation demonstrated an average memorandum generation time of 12–15 seconds, p95 latency below 30 seconds, p99 latency below 60 seconds, 99.5% Groq API uptime, and zero generation failures throughout production validation activities.



Beyond engineering implementation, I prepared detailed technical documentation supporting long-term maintainability of the memorandum generation system. Deliverables included a Memo Generation Quality Assurance Report, Groq API Integration Documentation, Memo Template and Style Guide, Service Level Agreement documentation, operational guidelines, and production validation reports. These documents established engineering standards that future contributors can use to maintain and enhance the system.



The internship significantly strengthened my understanding of enterprise Generative Artificial Intelligence, production software engineering, reliability engineering, prompt engineering, quality assurance methodologies, and technical communication. More importantly, it demonstrated that successful Artificial Intelligence systems depend not only on capable models but also on rigorous engineering practices, measurable validation, operational governance, and continuous improvement.
