## OBJECTIVE

Validate AI-generated underwriting memos using a standardized quality scoring framework and recommendation confidence model to ensure generated memos are complete, relevant, justified, actionable, and consistent for underwriting decisions.

===================================
1. MEMO QUALITY SCORING METHODOLOGY
===================================

Each memo was evaluated using five quality factors.

• Completeness (1 Point)

* Verified presence of all mandatory sections:

  * Applicant Profile
  * Risk Assessment
  * Credit History
  * Repayment Capacity
  * Risk Factors
  * Recommendation

• Clarity (1 Point)

* Memo content is readable, well-structured, and easy to understand.

• Relevance (1 Point)

* All information directly supports the underwriting recommendation.

• Justification (1 Point)

* Recommendation is supported by applicant credit profile, income, repayment capacity, and identified risk factors.

• Actionability (1 Point)

* Memo provides a clear decision that analysts can immediately act upon.

Quality Score = Completeness + Clarity + Relevance + Justification + Actionability

Quality Scale

5 = Excellent

4 = Strong

3 = Acceptable

2 = Weak

1 = Poor

======================================
2. RECOMMENDATION CONFIDENCE FRAMEWORK
======================================

Recommendation confidence represents the certainty of the underwriting decision.

High Confidence (95%)

* Strong credit profile
* Verified employment
* Complete documentation
* Low repayment risk

Medium Confidence (70%)

* Moderate credit profile
* Minor documentation gaps
* Additional verification recommended

Low Confidence (40%)

* Weak credit profile
* Employment uncertainty
* High repayment risk

During validation, API responses returned High, Medium, and Low confidence levels. These were validated against applicant risk profiles and mapped to 95%, 70%, and 40% respectively for reporting purposes.

===================
3. ESCALATION RULES
===================

Escalate for senior analyst review when:

* Quality Score < 2

OR

* Recommendation Confidence < 50%

Low-confidence recommendations were correctly identified and flagged for review.
